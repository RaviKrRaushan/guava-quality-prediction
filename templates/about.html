Research Design

Our research design outlines the systematic approach taken to develop and evaluate the video frame-based guava quality classification system. It encompasses data collection, preprocessing, model development, and rigorous evaluation phases.

Figure 3: Research Design Flowchart

The illustration above shows the fundamental research design and methodology undertook for the classification of guava quality into three classes namely; Fresh, Mid and Rotten. It begins with a video recording which involves a mobile device or a camera (Step 1).

In Step 2, frames are captured from the supplied video, and further processes such as rotation, flipping, zooming, and brightness adjustments are implemented to improve the overall dataset‚Äôs augmentation, diversity, and robustness.

Actual and augmented data is saved in Step 3 and then split in Training Set (70%) and Validation Set (30%) in Step 4. This split assists in enhancing the model generalization as well as averting overfitting.

In the last step, images are processed by Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) where features are extracted in convolution and pooling layers. Other than that, the model was able to classify the guava as ‚ÄòFresh‚Äô, ‚ÄòMid‚Äô or ‚ÄòRotten‚Äô based on predetermined features.

Revolutionary Approach

üéØ Key Innovation: First comprehensive study using 360¬∞ video frames for guava quality assessment, addressing the critical limitation of single-view image classification.

Traditional methods rely on manual sorting or single-view images, missing hidden defects. Our research introduces a video-based approach that captures guava fruits from all angles, ensuring comprehensive quality evaluation.

The system classifies guavas into three quality categories: Fresh, Mid-quality, and Rotten using advanced ensemble learning techniques that combine multiple deep learning models.

üìä Research Impact: Published methodology demonstrates superior accuracy compared to traditional single-image classification approaches, with ensemble models achieving 97.33% validation accuracy.

Advanced Technical Implementation

360¬∞ Video Capture

Records guava from all angles at 30 FPS in 1080P resolution, ensuring comprehensive visual assessment.

Frame Extraction

Extracts 5 evenly spaced frames per video, creating structured dataset with data augmentation techniques.

Ensemble Learning

Combines multiple models using soft voting, hard voting, and feature fusion strategies.

High Accuracy

Achieves 97.33% accuracy with NER ensemble model, outperforming individual architectures.
